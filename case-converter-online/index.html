Have you ever found yourself working on a document, making a few capitalization errors, and wishing you could save time on editing? The Toggle Case text tool, also known as the Change Case tool, is designed to switch the case of all letters in your text input. It converts uppercase letters to lowercase and vice versa.

This tool is particularly handy when text has been mistakenly typed in uppercase due to an inadvertent Caps Lock key activation. Rather than deleting the affected text, you can easily highlight it and convert it to the correct case. Moreover, the Toggle Case tool can enhance your headlines or headers, making them more eye-catching. After all, it's easier to notice "eBay" compared to "eBay."

Switch to Sentence Case

The Sentence Case text tool focuses on capitalizing only the first letter of the first word in a sentence or group of words, as well as proper nouns. It's a style often used by newspaper publishers in the United States and the United Kingdom for titles, headings, and news headlines. In the UK, it's widely adopted in various publications, including lists, bibliographies, and reference lists.

While most word processors can perform this task, our tool can help you check for grammatical errors before submitting your content to an editor.

  2. Visit ViewTools Website at https://viewtools.website/case-converter-online.
  3. Paste the copied text into the provided text area.

The Capitalize Word text tool capitalizes the first letter of every word in the input sentence, except for articles, conjunctions, and prepositions. This style, known as "Title Case," is commonly used for titles in text, where every word in the sentence begins with a capital letter.

While Title Case is frequently used for book titles, play titles, and movie titles, it's less common for articles. The choice largely depends on your writing style unless you're following strict guidelines that specify the headline style to use.
